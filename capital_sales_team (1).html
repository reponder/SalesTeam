What It's Like

You walk into a room. A professional plays the buyer. You have a few minutes to build rapport, uncover a need, and make your case. A panel of judges watches every move.

It's nerve-wracking the first time. It's addictive after that.

How It Works

From first practice
to competition floor.

01

Show up and start practicing

We meet weekly for live role play practice. No prior experience — just come ready to learn. You'll run real scenarios, get direct feedback, and improve faster than you expect.

02

Compete regionally

Your first competition is usually a regional event like OCSC or UTISC — a full day of live role plays with real judges. You'll leave with clarity on where you stand and what you're working toward.

03

Take the national stage

Strong performers earn spots at national competitions like NISC, competing against hundreds of students from programs across the country. This is where the résumé moments are made.

04

Build connections that follow you

The professionals judging your role play are there to find talent. Students who compete walk away with real contacts — and in some cases, internship and job conversations — well before graduation.

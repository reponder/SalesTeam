About SoML

You're here to
build something.

The School of Management and Leadership is your home for the next four years. We're not just here to teach you business concepts — we're here to help you become someone who leads, adapts, and makes things happen.

Dress the Part

Business professional:
what it actually means.

You'll need 1–2 business professional outfits ready to go — career fairs, interviews, presentations, and professional events can come up as early as October of your first semester. This is not business casual. Here's exactly what that looks like.

✅

Suit or matched blazer + pants/skirt — navy, black, charcoal, or gray

✅

Dress shirt, blouse, or polished shell — solid or subtle pattern, pressed and tucked

✅

Tie (when wearing a suit) — solid or striped, length to the belt, nothing novelty

✅

Dress shoes — polished and clean; closed-toe for formal settings; if wearing heels, keep them at a modest height (2–3 inches max) — you'll be on your feet all day

✅

Belt matches shoes in color and finish

✅

Minimal, polished accessories — simple jewelry, structured bag or portfolio

✅

Clothing fits well — nothing too tight or too loose; neat and wrinkle-free

❌ Not Business Professional
Jeans (any color) T-shirts or hoodies Athletic shoes Shorts Wrinkled or untucked shirts Graphic tees Leggings as pants Flip flops or sandals Strong cologne or perfume Novelty ties Visible undergarments Overly casual dresses

Your Toolkit

Technology
recommendations.

You'll use your laptop every single day — in class, for assignments, group projects, and presentations. Here's what you need to know before you buy anything.

Preferred Windows PC
✅

Full compatibility with all SoML coursework and software

✅

Full Microsoft 365 suite free through your Capital email — everything installs and runs natively

✅

Campus lab computers also run Windows — the experience will be seamless

Also Fine Mac
✅

Works well for the majority of coursework

✅

Microsoft 365 for Mac is available free through your Capital email

⚠️

Excel on Mac behaves slightly differently than the Windows version — some features, keyboard shortcuts, and add-ins work differently. You'll adjust, but it's worth knowing upfront.

Not Compatible Chromebook
❌

Chromebooks are not compatible with SoML coursework. They cannot run the Microsoft 365 desktop applications required for your courses — the web-only versions are not sufficient.

❌

If you currently have a Chromebook, plan to purchase a Windows PC or Mac before the semester begins.

Course Materials

Where to get
your books.

Your professors will list required textbooks and materials on each course syllabus during the first week of class. Always purchase your course materials through the Capital University Bookstore — search by course to make sure you get exactly the right edition.

Visit the Bookstore
🏫

Capital University Bookstore

Shop by course at capital.ecampus.com — the easiest way to confirm you're getting exactly the right edition. Offers new, used, rental, and digital options.

🔄

Rent Instead of Buy

For most courses, renting is significantly cheaper than buying — especially for textbooks you won't reference again after the semester. The bookstore and several third-party sites offer rentals.

📱

Digital / eBook Editions

Many titles are available digitally at a lower cost. Some courses use platforms like Cengage Unlimited, McGraw-Hill Connect, or Pearson MyLab — your professor will specify if required.
